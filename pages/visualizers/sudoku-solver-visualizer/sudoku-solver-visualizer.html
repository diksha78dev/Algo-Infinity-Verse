The Backtracking Paradigm

1. The Choice

The algorithm finds an empty cell and tries placing a number from 1 to 9. It checks if the number violates the Sudoku rules (row, column, or 3x3 subgrid constraints).

2. The Recursion

If the number is valid, it tentatively places it and recursively attempts to fill the next empty cell in the grid. It continues this deeper into the puzzle.

3. The Backtrack

If it reaches a point where no number from 1-9 is valid, it realizes its previous choice was wrong. It undoes the choice (backtracks) and tries the next possible number.
